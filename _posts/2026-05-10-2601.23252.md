By [David Yallup](https://yallup.github.io), Namu Kroupa, and [Will Handley](https://willhandley.co.uk) — published in [TMLR, May 2026](https://openreview.net/forum?id=5mF2eRl3gt). Open-source implementation: [`yallup/nss`](https://github.com/yallup/nss).

## What the paper does

How do you make nested sampling run on a GPU? The standard PolyChord-style slice-sampling step is intrinsically sequential — each likelihood call depends on the last — which leaves modern accelerators idle. We introduce **Nested Slice Sampling (NSS)**: a vectorised reformulation that uses hit-and-run slice updates batched across live points, together with a near-optimal rule for setting the slice width that makes per-step compute predictable enough to compile and parallelise. The result keeps PolyChord-quality evidence estimates while running at GPU throughput, and on hard multimodal targets it outperforms tempered-SMC baselines that currently dominate the parallel-inference literature. Validation spans synthetic mixtures, high-dimensional Bayesian problems, and Gaussian-process hyperparameter marginalisation on Mauna Loa CO₂ and Airline Passengers.
