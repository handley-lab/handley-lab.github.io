[arXiv:2603.26644](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26644) — *Automatic Laplace Collapsed Sampling: Scalable Marginalisation of Latent Parameters via Automatic Differentiation*.

By Toby Lovick, [David Yallup](https://orcid.org/0000-0003-4716-5817), and [Will Handley](https://willhandley.co.uk).

## What the paper does

Hierarchical Bayesian models in cosmology and beyond often carry hundreds or thousands of latent parameters per object — one per supernova, per quasar light curve, per source. Standard nested sampling scales roughly as the cube of the parameter dimension, putting the full posterior out of reach when the latent dimension is large. We introduce **Automatic Laplace Collapsed Sampling (ALCS)**: at each nested-sampling likelihood evaluation, the latent variables $z$ are collapsed to a scalar contribution via maximum-a-posteriori optimisation and a Laplace approximation around the mode, both computed by autodiff. The effective sampling dimension drops from $d_\theta + d_z$ to just $d_\theta$, making Bayesian evidence tractable for problems where a full hierarchical sample would otherwise require a decade of GPU time.
