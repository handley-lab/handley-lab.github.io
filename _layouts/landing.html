Core Framing

Revolution What it accelerates Examples

AI for Scientists The researcher Reducing overhead, building infrastructure, rapid tooling

Methodology

Supervised Execution

Foundation Models

General surrogates trained on large corpora. Useful for brainstorming, but physics is about compression, not just prediction.

Autonomous Agents

End-to-end generation of research artefacts. Science is a culture of critique and accountability, not a pipeline to automate.

Augmented Humans

Human intent and verification, AI implementation. We permit cheap generation, but we make selection expensive and deliberate.

80/20: most research time is consumed by tasks that are necessary but not intrinsically scientific. AI collapses the overhead; the scientist focuses on selection and interpretation.

Principles

Six Principles

Capture content

Transcribe, photograph, archive. Build a knowledge base that extends the context window.

Engineer context

Organise knowledge — skills, conventions, physics — rather than perfecting prompt syntax.

Specify physics, not code

State the physical intent. Treat generated code as a product to be tested.

Verify rigorously

Reference outputs, simulation-based calibration, adversarial review. Verification is the method.

Use multiple models

One implements, another critiques. No single channel of generation.

Stay the scientist

Define the questions, choose the methods, take responsibility for the answers.
